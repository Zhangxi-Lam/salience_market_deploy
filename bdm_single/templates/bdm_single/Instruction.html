Basic Idea

In the main part of the experiment, you will play 30 trading periods of an investment game. In each trading period, you will trade {{ num_assets }} risky asset with a computer investor. The return of the asset depends on its state. There are {{ num_states }} possible states. At the beginning of each trading period, you are informed of the number of all possible states, the probability of each state, and the return of the asset at each state.

During each trading period, you trade asset with the computer investor but do NOT know the true state of the asset. At the end of the trading period, the system tosses a 10-sided dice, and randomly assigns the true state of the asset depending on the probability of each state. Your payoff in each trading period depends on the number of assets you own and the true state of the asset.

For example, suppose that there are two states: state 1 with probability 40% and state 2 with probability 60%. You do NOT know which state will become the true state during the trading period. At the end of the trading period, the true state of each asset will be randomly determined based on the probabilities of the two states. Since the system uses a 10-sided fair dice, then throwing 1-4 means state 1 is chosen, while throwing 5-10 means state 2 is chosen.

How to Trade

At the beginning of the trading period, you are endowed with some cash and some assets. You need to set up a bid (the price you are willing to buy) and an ask (the price you are willing to sell). Your bid should not be higher than your ask, since you do not want to buy with a high price but sell with a low price. The computer investor then comes to the market with a random price for the asset you trade. The random price is a random integer between the maximum possible and the minimum possible return of the asset. The actual trade depends on the investor's random price, and your bid and ask.

For example, the investor comes with a price X for Asset A.

  • If X is not higher than your bid, then you will buy 1 unit of Asset A from the investor and lose X cash.
  • If X is not lower than your ask, then you will sell 1 unit of your Asset A to the investor and gain X cash.
  • If X is between your bid and ask, no trade will happen.

Suppose Asset A provides a 50% chance to get 200 and a 50% chance to get 0, then 0 is Asset A's minimum possible return and 200 is Asset A's maximum possible return. In this case,

  • The investor's random price X is an interger between 0 and 200.
  • You should not bid below 0 since 0 is your minimum possible return.
  • You should not ask above 200 since 200 is your maximum possible return.

When you enter your bid and ask, be sure to manually press next. Otherwise the system will not record your inputs.

Payment

At the end of the trading period, you will see a result page that summarizes your actions and performance. The result page reveals the true state of the assets, your final asset holding, your choices, and your payoff. Your total payoff will be your remaining cash + your earnings from your final asset holding. You can think of the cash as a risk-free asset which provides you a guaranteed 1:1 return, while the tradable asset are risky.

The figure below shows an example of the result page.

  • Asset A's state is 2. The assets will be paid based on their returns at the true states.
  • The player bids 80 and asks 120 for Asset A. The investor's price for Asset A is 116. Since 116 is between 80 and 120, no trade happens.
  • After the trade, the player possesses 1 unit of Asset A and 200 cash. So the final payoff is 100+200=300.

After the result page, all traders go on together to the next trading period.

At the end of the experiment, one non-practice trading period will be randomly chosen by the system, and your final payoff from this experiment is your payoff at that trading period (after converted to US dollar), plus your show-up fee. So your objective is to try to maximize your payoff at every non-practice trading period.

If you are ready for the experiment, please raise your hand. The experiment starts when everyone is ready.
